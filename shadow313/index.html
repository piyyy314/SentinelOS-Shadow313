How 313 Temporal Binding Works

Every quantum job receives a cryptographic proof of when it ran and what it computed. This is not just logging — it's verifiable provenance anchored to an immutable decentralized ledger.

1
Nanosecond Wait

SHADOW313 polls time.time_ns() until the timestamp ends in ...313 — happening every ~1ms, guaranteeing cryptographic uniqueness.

2
SHA-3 Signature Generation

A SHA3-256 hash is computed over: payload_hash + timestamp + sig_key — binding the data to the exact moment of computation.

3
Post-Quantum Signature

The signature is wrapped with a Dilithium-3 post-quantum key, future-proofing the binding against Shor's algorithm attacks on ECDSA.

4
IPFS Anchoring

The full bind record — including circuit hash, backend ID, Qiskit version, and results — is pinned to IPFS. The resulting CID is your permanent proof.
